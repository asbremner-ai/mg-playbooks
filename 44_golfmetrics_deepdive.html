Why Golfmetrics — and When

Arccos is your primary SG tracking tool. Golfmetrics is a quarterly supplement — the manual tracking method that captures what automatic systems cannot. It is not a replacement; it is a higher-resolution lens applied to specific rounds when maximum diagnostic precision is needed.

🔬 What Golfmetrics Captures That Arccos Misses
Automatic vs. Manual Tracking Comparison

The lie quality variable is Golfmetrics' most valuable unique contribution. Arccos cannot tell you whether a shot was from a perfect fairway lie or a plugged divot — Golfmetrics can. This allows you to separate skill variance from lie variance in your SG data.

⭐

When to use Golfmetrics: Quarterly, across 4 consecutive competitive rounds at the same course or courses where you have Arccos history. This allows direct comparison between automatic and manual data. Do not use Golfmetrics every round — the manual entry burden degrades decision quality if overused.

Data Collection System

The Golfmetrics data collection workflow is simple once practised — but the first two rounds will feel slow. Budget 15 minutes post-round for entry; experienced users do it in 10.

⚙️ Setup and Entry Protocol
Step-by-Step Collection Workflow

Round Data Entry

  • 1
    Account setup: Register at golfmetrics.com. Enter your baseline handicap index. The system uses this to calibrate your SG benchmarks against the appropriate reference set.
  • 2
    During the round — note-taking: On your scorecard, record three numbers per shot as a brief code: starting yardage (from GPS or sprinkler), lie quality (1=poor, 3=average, 5=perfect), shot outcome (G=green, F=fairway, R=rough, B=bunker, W=water). Example: "148/4/G" means 148-yard shot, good lie, hit the green.
  • 3
    Post-round entry (within 2 hours): Enter the coded data into Golfmetrics. The system converts shot sequences into SG calculations automatically. Total entry time with practice: 10–12 minutes.
  • 4
    Proximity notes: For approach shots only, optionally record step-count to the pin post-round for the 3 most instructive approaches of the day. This adds the proximity variable that Golfmetrics needs for its highest-resolution analysis.
📱 Practical Field Notes
Staying Efficient During Play

Not Slowing Your Round

The biggest concern with manual tracking is pace of play. Three rules that keep it clean:

  • 1
    Record notes while others are playing, not while it is your turn. The 30-second window while a playing partner putts is sufficient for a full shot notation.
  • 2
    Use a pre-printed pocket scorecard with the lie quality scale and outcome codes printed on the back. Eliminates any mental translation during the round.
  • 3
    If you miss a notation, leave it blank — do not guess retrospectively. Golfmetrics handles missing data better than incorrect data.

🧮 Benchmark Interpretation
Understanding Your Comparison Group

Who You Are Being Benchmarked Against

Golfmetrics benchmarks your data against its full user database — which skews toward serious amateurs and low handicappers (predominantly 0–5 HCP users). This means the "average" in Golfmetrics is already significantly better than general amateur average. Being at the Golfmetrics benchmark puts you at approximately low-single-figure standard. Beating the benchmark consistently puts you at plus-handicap standard.

Lie Quality as a Variable

The lie quality variable is Golfmetrics' most powerful unique feature. Separating skill variance from lie variance reveals whether your weak SG numbers come from technical imprecision or from genuinely bad fortune — the two look identical in Arccos data.

📊 The Lie Quality Analysis
Skill vs. Luck Separation

What Lie Quality Data Reveals

PatternInterpretation
Poor SG from lie quality 1–2 onlyNormal — difficult lies cost strokes for everyone. Not a skill issue.
Poor SG from lie quality 3–4 (average-good lies)Technical issue — you are losing strokes from lies where plus-handicap players do not. This is a fixable skill gap.
Poor SG from lie quality 5 (perfect lies)Decision-making issue — you are selecting the wrong targets or clubs from ideal positions. Review Guide 40 (Decision Architecture).
Dramatically worse SG from rough vs. fairwayNormal — but if the gap is >1.2 SG/shot at your level, rough contact quality needs specific work.
🔬 Practical Application
Using Lie Data in Practice Design

Translating Lie Quality Findings to Training

  • 1
    If losing strokes from lie quality 3 (average lies), prioritise consistent ball-striking from ordinary conditions before spending time on specialist lie practice. The baseline is not solid enough.
  • 2
    If losing strokes from lie quality 5 (perfect lies), this is a decision-making problem not a technical one. Review your course management decisions post-round — are you selecting aggressive targets that are producing poor outcomes even when executed correctly?
  • 3
    Use your lie quality SG data in coaching sessions. Show your coach the specific lie quality where your SG breaks down. This focuses the technical conversation far more precisely than "I'm struggling with my irons."

The Quarterly Golfmetrics Audit

The Golfmetrics quarterly audit is a structured 4-round data collection protocol that extracts maximum diagnostic value while keeping the manual tracking burden manageable. Four rounds in comparable conditions produce statistically reliable findings.

📋 Quarterly Audit Protocol
4-Round Data Collection

Structure and Timing

AspectSpecification
FrequencyOnce per quarter — 4 times per year
Rounds4 competitive rounds (not practice rounds)
Course selectionSame course(s) as prior quarter where possible for comparability
Entry windowEnter data within 2 hours of each round; do not batch-enter multiple rounds later
Analysis timingAfter all 4 rounds are entered — not after each round
🔍 12-Question Quarterly Analysis
Extracting Maximum Insight

The Post-Audit Review Framework

  • 1
    Consistency check: Is the pattern consistent across all 4 rounds, or driven by 1 outlier round? Patterns in 3+ of 4 rounds are real. Patterns in 1 round are noise.
  • 2
    Distance band priority: Which 25-yard band produced the worst SG per shot? That is the training focus for the next 6 weeks.
  • 3
    Lie quality separator: Are losses coming from poor lies (normal) or from average/good lies (technical problem)? This single question determines whether you need technical coaching or decision-making coaching.
  • 4
    Quarter-on-quarter delta: Compare this quarter's SG averages to the prior quarter. If APP has not improved after a 12-week APP-focused training block, either the technical work is not translating (coach conversation needed) or it has improved but is masked by course difficulty variance (compare same-course rounds only).

Multi-Season Trend Analysis

For players with 2+ years of quarterly Golfmetrics data, multi-season trend analysis becomes possible. This is the most valuable analytical work available to a serious amateur — and almost none of them do it.

📈 Long-Term Pattern Recognition
What 8+ Quarters of Data Reveals

Multi-Season Analysis Framework

  • 1
    Identify plateau points: Plot SG per category per quarter across 8 quarters. Most players see one or two categories plateau first — typically ARG and Putting plateau before APP at elite level. The plateau is where your training investment is returning zero, and a coaching intervention is needed.
  • 2
    Seasonal cycles: Many players show consistent SG variance by season — stronger APP in summer (more range time, better weather), stronger putting in winter (more indoor practice). Knowing your seasonal patterns allows you to plan training blocks that counter natural drift.
  • 3
    Training block attribution: Cross-reference your SG deltas with your training log. Which 12-week blocks produced the largest SG gains? Those are your highest-return training activities — repeat them.
