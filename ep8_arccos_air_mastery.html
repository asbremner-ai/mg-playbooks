Why Data Quality Matters More Than Data Volume

The Garbage-In Problem

A player who plays 60 rounds per year with Arccos tracking but has consistent misattribution errors — shots attributed to the wrong club, chips registered as putts, chip-and-runs counted as approach shots — will accumulate 60 rounds of misleading SG data. Their SG: Putting will appear worse than reality because chip-and-runs are classified as putts; their SG: ARG will appear better than reality for the same reason.

The effort required to correct these attribution errors is small — 2–3 minutes per round immediately post-round. The improvement in data reliability is substantial. One year of clean data is worth more than three years of dirty data.

Setup & Calibration

The one-time and periodic configuration steps that determine the accuracy of all Arccos Air data going forward. Most players set up Arccos once and never revisit it. This is a mistake — calibration degrades as your distances change and as new features are released.

⚙️ Initial Configuration
Getting Arccos Air Right from the Start

The 6-Step Setup Protocol

  • 1
    Club configuration — add every club you carry. Include all 14 clubs plus the putter. Common error: omitting hybrid or fairway wood because "I don't use it much." Arccos uses your full bag for shot classification — a missing club forces incorrect attribution to adjacent clubs in every round you carry it.
  • 2
    Disable "Automatic Club Detection" initially. For your first 5 rounds with Arccos Air, manually confirm each shot attribution immediately after the round. This builds an understanding of where Air tracking makes systematic errors — specific shot types and distances where automatic detection is least reliable for your game.
  • 3
    Set your handicap accurately before the first round. Arccos uses your handicap to calibrate expected performance benchmarks. An inaccurate handicap skews every SG calculation for your profile. Update it immediately after any index change — do not wait for the app to sync automatically.
  • 4
    Enable Smart Club Distances and let it run for 20 rounds. Smart Club Distances updates your average carry per club based on tracked rounds. The distances after 20 rounds are your actual carries in real-course conditions — more reliable than any Mevo range session for on-course decision-making. Do not override them with your range session distances.
  • 5
    Competition mode — understand when to use it. Competition mode disables the AI Caddie recommendations (required under USGA/R&A rules for club-level competition in the UK unless the club explicitly permits it). Confirm your club's local rule before each competition round — failure to use competition mode where required is a Rules violation.
  • 6
    Enable automatic round detection and test it. Walk the first three holes of a practice round with Arccos running in the background without manually starting. Confirm detection is triggering. If it consistently fails to auto-detect, switch to manual round start — the data from a missed detection round is unrecoverable.

Shot Attribution & Correction

The single most important data quality practice. Arccos Air misattributes shots in predictable, correctable ways. Understanding where errors occur and fixing them immediately post-round is the difference between reliable and unreliable SG data.

🔧 The Attribution Error Map

The Post-Round Correction Protocol

2–3 Minutes That Protect Your Data Quality

  • 1
    Review the round in the Arccos app within 1 hour of finishing. Memory of specific shots degrades quickly. Hole by hole, review the shot list. Any shot with an implausible distance (e.g. a 3-yard approach) or wrong club attribution is flagged for correction.
  • 2
    Correct club attributions first. Tap any shot to edit the club. For shots where you used a club different from what Arccos detected, update it. Pay particular attention to transition distances and chips.
  • 3
    Delete phantom shots. Any shot that appears in the round that you didn't play — GPS glitches, accidental detection — delete it. A round with one phantom shot has corrupted SG data for every category; deletion restores it.
  • 4
    Verify the shot count against your actual scorecard. Compare the total number of Arccos-recorded shots to your scorecard total. If they differ by more than 2, there is a systematic error in the round data that requires detailed review.
  • 5
    Add notes for unusual lies or conditions. Arccos doesn't know if your approach was from a divot or a heavy rough lie. Add a note for any hole where conditions explain a below-standard shot outcome — this context is valuable in the monthly data review.

The AI Caddie — What It's Based On

The Arccos AI Caddie makes club recommendations based on a combination of your Smart Club Distances, course GPS data, and population data from millions of tracked shots. Understanding what it is — and what it isn't — allows you to use it intelligently rather than blindly.

🤖 How the AI Caddie Works
The Recommendation Engine — Under the Hood

What the AI Caddie Is Actually Calculating

The AI Caddie combines three data sources for each recommendation: your personal Smart Club Distances (personalised), GPS-mapped course data for the specific hole (course-specific), and aggregated data from how players with similar handicaps have played that shot type on similar holes (population data).

The output is a club recommendation that maximises expected proximity to the hole across the population of players who have played similar shots. It is not personalised to your miss pattern, your trajectory preferences, or your specific lie. It is a good-faith population-average recommendation, not a bespoke caddie decision.

What AI Caddie Accounts ForWhat It Doesn't Account For
Your personal carry distances (Smart Club Distances)Your dominant miss direction
Distance to the flagToday's wind conditions (unless you input wind)
Hazards on the carry lineGreen firmness and run-out
Uphill/downhill adjustments (partially)Your current form or fatigue level
Safe side of the green vs flagSpecific lie type (rough, tight, slope)
When to Follow and When to Override

A Decision Framework for AI Caddie Recommendations

SituationAI Caddie ReliabilityRecommendation
Standard approach, flat lie, calm conditionsHigh — population data is reliable hereFollow it unless you have specific miss-pattern knowledge that contradicts it
Into strong headwind (>15 mph)Medium — wind adjustment is approximateAdd one club to the AI recommendation; it systematically under-clubs into wind
Significant rough lieLow — lie is not modelledOverride; a rough lie requires less club (flyer risk) or more club (heavy rough) depending on lie type
Forced carry over water with your dominant miss direction toward the waterLow — miss direction is not modelledOverride; apply your personal dispersion data and take more club if your miss threatens the penalty area
Tee shot club selectionMedium — course management is partially modelledCompare AI recommendation to your pre-committed strategy card (Guide 40). The strategy card takes precedence on holes you have pre-planned; use AI on unfamiliar holes.
Unfamiliar course (first time playing)High — course data is reliableFollow it more closely; it has course-specific hazard data you don't have
⭐

Competition mode compliance: In all club strokeplay competitions, Stableford events, and county qualifying events in the UK, the AI Caddie constitutes an artificial intelligence device providing advice. It must be disabled unless the Committee has specifically permitted it. When in doubt, disable it and rely on your own pre-committed strategy cards.

Analytics Deep-Dive

The specific Arccos analytics screens that produce the highest-value practice decisions — beyond the headline SG dashboard that Guide 26 covers. These are the second and third level of the analytics hierarchy that most players never reach.

📊 The Analytics Hierarchy
Three Levels of Arccos Analytics

From Dashboard to Decision

  • 1
    Level 1 — SG Dashboard (covered in Guide 26): Five-category SG overview, 10-round rolling average, trend lines. The starting point for practice prioritisation. Most players stop here.
  • 2
    Level 2 — Category Deep-Dives (this guide): Within each SG category, Arccos provides sub-category analytics that reveal where within the category the losses are occurring. For SG: APP, this means proximity by distance band. For SG: Putting, this means make percentage by distance bracket. For SG: OTT, this means fairway hit rate vs. distance profile. These sub-category analytics are where practice decisions become specific rather than general.
  • 3
    Level 3 — Situational Analytics: Specific situations — approaches from rough vs. fairway, putts after chip vs. approach, par 5 scoring relative to field. These require sufficient sample size (40+ rounds) but reveal the game construction insights that sub-category analytics cannot.

Exporting Data for External Analysis

Getting Your Data Out of Arccos

  • 1
    CSV export (web dashboard): Log in to arccosgolf.com on desktop. Navigate to Game Tracker → Export. Select date range (full season). Download CSV. This file contains every tracked shot with club, distance, and hole data — the raw material for Golfmetrics and the multi-season analysis in Guide EP2.
  • 2
    What the CSV contains: Shot ID, date, hole number, shot number, club, distance to pin (start), distance to pin (end), lie type (if available), and GPS coordinates. The distance-to-pin data is the primary input for proximity analysis in Guide EP4.
  • 3
    Golfmetrics import: Golfmetrics accepts Arccos CSV exports directly. Navigate to Golfmetrics → Import Data → Arccos. Follow the field mapping guide. The SG calculations in Golfmetrics are derived from the same Broadie dataset as Arccos but with additional filtering options and multi-season visualisation capabilities not available in the Arccos app itself.

The Diagnostic Chain

Arccos → Mevo → HackMotion → Sportsbox — in Sequence

  • 1
    Arccos identifies the category and distance band where strokes are being lost. Example: SG: APP at 130–150 yards is the largest gap, with a dominant right miss in that bracket.
  • 2
    Mevo diagnoses ball flight. Hit 20 shots at 140 yards on the range. Mevo data shows: spin axis consistently positive (+4° to +6°) — the ball is fading right consistently, not randomly. The problem is directional, not distance.
  • 3
    HackMotion diagnoses wrist mechanics. HackMotion shows lead wrist is cupping (extending) through impact — opening the face. This is the clubface cause of the positive spin axis Mevo identified.
  • 4
    Sportsbox AI (if available) diagnoses body pattern. Sportsbox shows early extension — hips thrusting toward the ball in the downswing — which is causing the wrist to cup as a compensation. The root cause is the body pattern, not the wrist.
  • 5
    Coach session addresses root cause. Armed with the full diagnostic chain, the coaching session targets the hip extension pattern — the root cause — rather than the wrist position (a symptom) or the fade (the visible outcome).

Monthly Arccos Protocol

A structured monthly Arccos review that extracts maximum value from your tracked rounds in 30 minutes — the specific screens to visit, the questions to ask, and the single practice priority to derive from each monthly review.

📅 The 30-Minute Monthly Review
Step-by-Step Monthly Protocol

From Data to Practice Priority in 30 Minutes

  • 1
    Data quality check (5 min): Review the shot count from the most recent 5 rounds vs. your scorecard totals. Verify no phantom rounds or uncorrected attribution errors. If data quality issues exist, correct them before any analysis.
  • 2
    Rolling average check (5 min): Check your 10-round rolling SG average for all five categories. Apply the variance filter from Guide EP2 — only act on changes that have persisted for 3+ months. Note any category that has been below its trend for 3 consecutive months.
  • 3
    Category deep-dive on the priority category (10 min): Navigate to the analytics for your current practice priority category. For approach play: proximity by distance band. For putting: make% by distance. Identify the specific sub-category driving the SG gap.
  • 4
    Smart Club Distances verification (5 min): Check that Smart Club Distances have updated correctly for the season. Compare to your Mevo session baseline. Any club where the two systems diverge by more than 5 yards requires a diagnostic range session to determine which is correct.
  • 5
    Single practice priority output (5 min): Derive one specific, actionable practice priority from the monthly review. Not "improve my putting" but "increase my make% from 5–6 feet — currently 38%, target 45% — through the specific left-lip miss pattern that appears in 60% of misses in that bracket." Write it in the Progress Journal (Guide 17). Act on it for the next 4 weeks before reviewing again.
The Annual Arccos Archive

Building a Multi-Season Record

At the end of each season (October), export the full year's Arccos CSV and store it alongside the previous years' exports. This archive is the raw material for the multi-season analysis in Guide EP2 — the improvement trajectory modelling, regression analysis, and category correlation work that requires 2+ years of data. Without the annual archive, that analysis is unavailable.

Label each file clearly: arccos_export_2026_season.csv. Store in cloud storage (Google Drive, iCloud) not only on the phone — phone loss deletes your entire historical Arccos archive if you haven't backed up the exports.
