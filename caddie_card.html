Aim Point Protocol

Driver

Aim to the edge of the fairway in the direction of your miss. If you miss right, aim right-centre of the fairway — never left rough to play a draw. The dispersion cone is your constraint.

Iron Approaches

Middle of the green unless the flag is in the open half AND you have at least 15 yards of green between flag and hazard. Tucked pins: always play to the fat side. Sucker pins: never attack.

Scoring Zone (<100 yds)

Choose the matrix distance that matches your required yardage. No improvised swings. If the required distance falls between two matrix entries, play the shorter one and allow for run.

Decision Framework

Step 1 — Assess

Lie → distance → wind → pin category → slope. Complete this in the same order every shot. Missing a step costs shots — usually wind or lie assessment.

Step 2 — Select

Use your matrix for wedge shots. Apply wind formula for approaches above 10 mph. Select aim point based on your dispersion cone, not the flag. Always take one more club than instinct says — you almost certainly under-club.

Step 3 — Commit

Pre-shot routine. Full commitment. Trust the number. The club selected after a complete assessment process is always the right club — don't second-guess at address.

Never Do These

  • 🚫
    Attack a sucker pin. One hero shot per round erases 3 good shots. Always take the fat side.
  • 🚫
    Under-club to a green with carry trouble. Take the club that gets you to the BACK of the green. Balls short of the green lose more strokes than balls over.
  • 🚫
    Skip the wind adjustment when it's "not that windy". 10 mph into adds 15 yards at 150. Always apply the formula.
  • 🚫
    Change the club selection at address. If you need to change clubs, step away and restart the routine. Never change at address.
  • 🚫
    Play a recovery shot heroically. From trouble, the target is: back in play, in the best possible position. Not birdies. Bogey elimination wins more shots than birdie attempts.
  • 🚫
    Skip the pre-shot routine when nervous. The routine exists specifically for when you are nervous. Abbreviating it under pressure is the most common cause of competition blow-ups.

Always Do These

  • ✅
    Aim using your dispersion cone. Not the flag. The flag is a reference point. Your aim point is where the centre of your cone needs to be.
  • ✅
    Apply the wind formula above 10 mph. Every single time. Build it as automatic habit by Round 20.
  • ✅
    Complete the pre-shot routine. On every shot. Including tap-ins. The routine is the protection against the unconscious shortcut that produces double bogeys.
  • ✅
    Accept the shot you just hit. Move forward in your routine immediately. Carrying the previous shot into the next decision contaminates the assessment process.
  • ✅
    Hit the percentage shot from trouble. Sideways or backwards to a good lie is always the right play. Calculate the expected score from both options if needed — the conservative option wins.

Double-Bogey Elimination Protocol

When in Trouble

Ask: what shot makes a double bogey impossible? Not what shot makes birdie possible — what removes the worst outcome? Execute that shot. At 10 HCP, 3.2 double bogeys per round are the single biggest scoring leak. Eliminate one per round and your handicap drops immediately.
