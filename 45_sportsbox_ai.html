What Sportsbox AI Measures

Sportsbox AI converts a standard smartphone video into a 3D motion analysis of your swing — measuring variables that 2D video analysis cannot capture. At plus-handicap level, the movement differences that separate players are subtle and largely invisible to the naked eye. Sportsbox makes them quantifiable.

🎬 3D Variables Sportsbox Captures
Beyond 2D Video

Key Measurements at Elite Level

MetricWhat It MeasuresWhy It Matters
Pelvis Sway / ThrustLateral and forward pelvis movement at transition and impactExcess sway creates path variability; insufficient thrust reduces power transfer
Thorax Side-BendLateral tilt of the upper body at impactElite players typically reach 35–45° side-bend; insufficient side-bend produces high shots and thin contact

⭐

HackMotion comparison: HackMotion measures lead wrist position (face control). Sportsbox measures body motion (sequencing and power). They answer different questions — HackMotion tells you what the club face is doing; Sportsbox tells you why. Used together, they provide a complete picture of ball-striking patterns.

Setup and Data Capture

Sportsbox accuracy is more sensitive to camera position than 2D video apps. A correctly positioned phone produces research-grade 3D data. An incorrectly positioned phone produces plausible-looking but unreliable numbers. Follow the setup protocol exactly.

📱 Camera Setup Protocol
Position Requirements for Reliable Data

Camera Position Specifications

ParameterSpecification
Camera angle (face-on)Exactly perpendicular to target line — 0° deviation or data degrades significantly
Camera heightHip height of the player being filmed (approximately 36–40 inches from ground)
Distance from player12–15 feet — close enough to capture full body, far enough for limb tracking accuracy
Down-the-line angleDirectly behind the player's target line — not offset left or right
LightingEven natural light — avoid strong shadows across the player at impact position
BackgroundPlain or non-distracting — moving backgrounds (trees in wind) reduce tracking accuracy
🔄 Baseline Capture Session
First Session Protocol

Building Your Sportsbox Baseline

  • 1
    Warm up thoroughly first: Sportsbox data from cold swings does not represent your competitive movement patterns. Hit 20–30 balls before beginning the capture session.
  • 2
    Driver — 8 swings: Full effort, target at 250 yards. This gives the platform data for power and sequence analysis. Upload all 8; use the Sportsbox average view, not individual swings, for your baseline numbers.
  • 3
    7-iron — 8 swings: Same protocol. The 7-iron data is more consistent and reveals technical patterns more clearly than driver data for most amateur players.
  • 4
    Record the session date and conditions: Temperature, wind, range surface. Sportsbox data is most useful when compared to prior sessions in similar conditions.

📈 The Sequencing Graph
The Most Diagnostic Single View

How to Read Kinematic Sequence

The sequencing graph shows four lines — pelvis, thorax, arm, club — each peaking in velocity at different points in the downswing. The ideal pattern has each segment peak after the one below it, in a fluid wave from ground to clubhead.

PatternDiagnosisCommon Cause
Thorax peaks before pelvisOver-the-top tendency; path problemTransition too fast with upper body; insufficient hip lead
Arm peaks before thoraxEarly release; thin/fat patternCasting — arm extension before body rotation completes
All segments bunched togetherPoor separation — power leakageBody rotation too slow overall; Rypstick protocol needed
Clean cascade, correct orderOptimal sequenceN/A — maintain and monitor

Integration with Your Coach

Sportsbox is most valuable as a coach communication tool — it gives your coach data they cannot see from 2D video. The most effective use is to share Sportsbox data before a lesson, not during one.

🎓 Pre-Lesson Protocol
How to Use Sportsbox to Maximise Lesson Value

The Pre-Lesson Sharing Process

  • 1
    3–5 days before the lesson: Capture a Sportsbox session. Export the session summary — Sportsbox allows PDF or link export from the app dashboard.
  • 2
    Send to your coach: Include the four priority metrics (X-Factor, pelvis velocity, thorax velocity, lead arm rotation rate) and the sequencing graph. One paragraph of context: "My ball-striking from 150 yards has been inconsistent — I'm hitting it 5 feet further right than I should be. Here is this week's Sportsbox data."
  • 3
    In the lesson: The coach arrives with data already reviewed. The lesson starts with a hypothesis ("I think your thorax is peaking before your pelvis") and spends 50 minutes testing and fixing it, not 15 minutes diagnosing it first.
🔍 Finding a Sportsbox-Certified Coach
Working with Data-Literate Coaches

Sportsbox Coach Directory

Sportsbox maintains a directory of coaches certified in their platform at sportsboxai.com/coaches. Certified coaches have completed Sportsbox's training programme and can interpret 3D data directly without requiring you to translate it for them.

If your current coach is not Sportsbox-certified, the most efficient approach is to share the four priority metrics and the sequencing graph image only — these are self-explanatory to any coach who understands biomechanics, regardless of platform familiarity.

🔄 The Combined Session Workflow
Monthly Three-Tool Session

Sequenced Data Collection

  • 1
    Sportsbox first (15 min): 8 driver, 8 7-iron with camera positioned correctly. Upload and note the four priority metrics and sequencing graph.
  • 2
    HackMotion second (15 min): 10 driver swings with HackMotion running. Note lead wrist position at the top and at impact. Cross-reference with the Sportsbox X-Factor and lead arm rotation rate — they should correlate predictably.
  • 3
    Mevo third (15 min): 10 driver shots with full Mevo data capture. Note smash factor, lateral offset, and spin rate. If lateral dispersion is outside target (Guide 41 OTT section), trace the cause using the HackMotion and Sportsbox data from steps 1 and 2.
  • 4
    Post-session synthesis: Write 3 sentences in your Progress Journal: what the data showed, what it suggests technically, and what coaching question it generates for your next lesson.
