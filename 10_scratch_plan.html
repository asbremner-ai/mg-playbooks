Practice Session Design

How Each Session Must Be Structured

Every practice session follows the 30/35/20/10/5 split from the existing plan — but must include pass/fail criteria on every drill. Random practice over block practice for 78% vs. 42% skill retention at 72 hours. Maximum deliberate practice: 60–90 minutes. Beyond that, skill acquisition drops to near zero.

  • 30%Putting — Speed gates, Clock Drill, Make 100 at 4 ft, AimPoint green reading. Track make rate and 3-putt rate.
  • 35%Short Game — Proximity tracking from standard chips, full wedge matrix, bunker saves. Pass/fail: chip to 8-ft circle.
  • 20%Irons — Low Point Drill, dispersion pattern work on launch monitor. Target: <30 ft from 150 yds.
  • 10%Driver — SuperSpeed if speed is a limiter, course simulation off tee. Track fairways hit %.
  • 5%Mental — Pressure reps, pre-shot routine under consequence, post-session data review.
Post-Round Protocol

10 Minutes That Compound Over Time

  • 1Open Arccos or Shot Scope. Review your SG in all four categories for the round.
  • 2Identify the single biggest leak. Record it in your game log.
  • 3Set the focus for your next practice session based on what the data shows — not what feels bad emotionally.
  • 4Apply the 10-second rule: close the round completely. The next round gets a clean slate.
⚠️

Critical rule: Never practise swing mechanics on the day of a round. Pre-round putting protocol (20-min tour warm-up) is the only permitted practice. Mechanical thinking on the course destroys automatic motor programmes and is the primary cause of performance regression under pressure.

The Roadmap

FOUR PHASES TO SCRATCH

Phases 1–3 from the existing programme take you to +1–2 by Month 12. Phase 4 is new — it closes the final margin through elite execution, precision standards, and competitive exposure.

Phase1

Stop the Bleeding

Months 1–2 · Target: Handicap ~8 · Focus: Eliminate double bogeys
  • ✓Install and use pre-shot routine on every single shot — no exceptions.
  • ✓Begin tracking 5 stats per round via Arccos or Shot Scope from Round 1.
  • ✓Apply shot selection hierarchy: no hero plays from trouble. Chip out, take your medicine.
  • ✓Aim for the fat part of the green on all pins that are not clearly accessible.
  • ✓Begin 6-minute daily mobility routine: thoracic, hip 90/90, hip flexor, wrist.
📊

Expected result: −2 to −3 strokes in scoring average within 6–8 rounds. Most of this gain comes from decision-making, not ball-striking.

Phase2

Build the Foundation

Months 3–4 · Target: Handicap ~7 · Focus: Short game + putting confidence
  • ✓Structured short game practice every session — track proximity, not feel.
  • ✓Build the full wedge distance matrix: all 4 clubs × 4 clock positions (8 o'clock to 12 o'clock).
  • ✓Make 100 Drill weekly at 4 feet — build neurological confidence under pressure.
  • ✓Get a professional putter fitting — highest ROI equipment investment available (+0.3–0.8 SG).
  • ✓Begin strength training 2× per week: hip hinge focus (deadlifts, hip thrusts, goblet squats).
🎯

Expected result: Up-and-down rate improving toward 35%+. 3-putt rate below 10%. Handicap approaching 7–8.

Phase3

Build the Attack

Months 5–12 · Target: Handicap ~1–2 · Focus: Approach play + GIR
  • ✓Launch monitor session: establish verified carry distances for every club. End under-clubbing permanently.
  • ✓Add Alignment Stick Low Point Drill to every iron practice session. Target consistent ball-first contact.
  • ✓Apply wind percentage system on every approach where wind exceeds 10 mph. Automate it until it is unconscious.
  • ✓Begin SuperSpeed training if driver speed is a limiter: 3× per week, 10 minutes per session.
  • ✓Apply dispersion-based targeting on all approach shots — aim based on your shot pattern, not the flag.
  • ✓Enter at least 1 competitive round per month (club medal, stableford, or equivalent).
📈

Expected result: GIR% improving toward 40–45%. Approach proximity under 30 ft from 150 yards. Handicap reaching +1–2 by Month 12.

Phase4

Elite Execution — The Scratch Standard

Months 13–24 · Target: Scratch · Focus: Precision, competition, closing the margin
  • ✓Approach proximity target: <25 ft from 150 yards. GIR% target: 55–60%. These are scratch-calibre standards — tighten from Phase 3 targets.
  • ✓Eliminate bogeys from miscalculation, not poor ball-striking. Every decision on course must be the statistically correct one, not the emotionally compelling one.
  • ✓Quarterly swing audits with a TPI-certified coach. Your SG data tells you what is leaking; a coach tells you why and how to fix it efficiently. Self-directed improvement has a ceiling around +2–3.
  • ✓Up-and-down rate target: 40%+. Bunker save rate: 35%+. 3-putt rate: <5%. 5-ft make rate: 85%+. These are the scratch benchmarks — not aspirational, but required.
  • ✓Minimum 2 competitive rounds per month. Club championship, open competitions, county events. Pressure exposure is non-negotiable at scratch level. Your handicap will not reflect your capability until you perform under real stakes consistently.
  • ✓Weekly pressure practice — putting for consequence, chipping to tight targets with a financial penalty for misses. Simulate the emotional conditions of competition in every practice session.
  • ✓Full physical protocol sustained: strength 2×/week, cardio 2–3×/week, daily mobility, sleep discipline, on-course nutrition. At scratch level, physical precision across 18 holes under competitive pressure is a primary differentiator.
🏆

Expected result: Scratch handicap confirmed across multiple competitive rounds. All four SG categories within scratch benchmarks simultaneously. This is the standard — not aspirational, but achievable with the commitment you have made.

Physical Development

THE ATHLETIC FOUNDATION

Scratch requires sustaining physical precision and mental focus across 18 holes under competitive pressure. The fitness protocol is not supplementary — it is a core pillar of the plan. Golfers who train structured physical programmes improve 35% faster and sustain improvement longer.

The Four Non-Negotiable Movements

  • 1
    Hip hinge (deadlift pattern): Posterior chain power — the engine of ground force and rotational speed.
  • 2
    Anti-rotation core (Pallof press): Stabilises the spine under load. Prevents energy leak between lower and upper body.
  • 3
    Thoracic rotation: Directly caps shoulder turn. Restricted thoracic rotation cannot be compensated by technique alone.
  • 4
    Single-leg stability: Golf is played on one leg at impact. Balance under load = consistency under fatigue.

Speed Target for Scratch

  • ▸
    Every 1 mph of added driver speed adds approximately 2.5 yards of carry.
  • ▸
    Scratch benchmark driver speed: 108–115 mph. If currently 95–100 mph, a 10–15 mph gain is achievable in 12–24 months.
  • ▸
    SuperSpeed protocol: 3× per week, 10 minutes, produces 4–8% speed gains in 4–6 weeks.

Fuel the Performance

NUTRITION FOR SCRATCH

Cognitive performance — decision quality on holes 14–18 — is almost entirely determined by blood glucose stability and hydration. Scratch golfers maintain both across 4+ hours. Most amateurs do not eat or drink enough to sustain decision quality beyond the 12th hole.

The On-Course Protocol — Non-Negotiable

  • ▸
    Pre-round (2 hrs before): Low-GI meal. Oats, eggs, wholegrain toast. Stable blood glucose from tee to 18.
  • ▸
    Hydration: 500ml in the hour before teeing off. Sip 200–250ml every 4–5 holes. Electrolytes in heat. Even 1.5% dehydration reduces cognitive function 10–15%.
  • ▸
    On-course: Small low-GI snacks every 5–6 holes — banana, nuts, energy bar. Never wait until ravenous.
  • ▸
    Caffeine: 100–200mg (1–2 coffees), 45 minutes before the round. More than 300mg produces anxiety and tremor that impairs putting.

Competitive Exposure

YOU MUST COMPETE

This is the most commonly missed element in a self-directed improvement programme. Your handicap will not accurately reflect your ability until you perform consistently under real competitive pressure. Pressure is a skill — and it must be trained like any other.

"Practice puts your brain in a state of controlled comfort. Competition puts it in a state of controlled discomfort. You need both — but scratch golfers have learned to perform in the second state." — Dr. Gio Valiante, sports psychologist to Dustin Johnson
Why Competitive Play Is Non-Negotiable

The Pressure Gap at +2 to Scratch

The single most common reason golfers stall at +2–3 handicap is that they have never regularly played under the conditions that produce pressure. A Saturday fourball is not competitive pressure. A club medal where your handicap certificate hangs on the result is. The neuroscience is clear: motor programmes that work in practice will fail under novel stress unless they have been repeatedly tested under pressure and reinforced.

  • ▸Year 1 minimum: 1 competitive round per month — club medal, open stableford, or equivalent counted competition.
  • ▸Year 2 minimum: 2 competitive rounds per month. Enter the club championship. Enter at least one open amateur event. Enter a county qualifier if accessible.
  • ▸Practice pressure simulation: Every session must include one block of pressure reps — putting for £1 per miss, chipping to tight targets with a consequence for failure, playing a simulated back-nine in practice with a scoring standard to hit.
Mental Game Under Pressure

The Three Routines That Must Hold

Three mental habits separate a +2 golfer from a scratch golfer under pressure. These must be trained until they are completely automatic — not aspirational behaviours that fall apart when the stakes rise.

  • 1Pre-shot routine without deviation: Same sequence, same duration (20–28 seconds), same trigger, every shot. Tour research shows pre-shot routine consistency is the #1 predictor of performance under pressure. If your routine changes when nervous, it is not yet a routine.
  • 210-second bad shot rule: Allow 10 seconds of honest reaction to a bad shot. After that, the door closes. Any longer than 15 seconds of rumination measurably impairs the next shot. Build the habit in practice so it is automatic in competition.
  • 3Process goals only on the course: Set scoring aspirations before the round, then forget them entirely on the course. Outcome goals ("I need to birdie this hole") trigger anxiety that disrupts automatic execution. Process goals ("I'm going to commit fully to this routine") keep attention where performance lives.
Competitive Mindset — Self-Talk

The Language You Use Changes What You Do

Research by Dr. Antonis Hatzigeorgiadis shows that instructional self-talk ("smooth tempo," "stay down") improves execution of skilled motor tasks by 12–18% compared to silence. Negative self-talk ("don't hit it in the water") impairs performance by 15–22% — because your brain processes the image of the feared outcome. Build a vocabulary of positive, process-focused cue words for each shot type. Use them deliberately in competition — they are not affirmations, they are performance tools.
