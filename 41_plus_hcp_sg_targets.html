The Plus-Handicap Benchmarks

Scratch is not the end of the journey — it is the beginning of a different one. The gaps between scratch and +1/+2/+3 are smaller in aggregate than the gaps that got you here, but they sit in specific, high-difficulty areas that require targeted work to close.

Key insight: APP is the largest single gap at every step of the plus journey. A player who reaches scratch typically has SG: APP near 0.0. The fastest route to +1 is almost always approach play — specifically proximity compression from 125–175 yards.

🔢 How to Use This Data
Diagnostic Protocol

Finding Your Biggest Gap

  • 1
    Pull your Arccos season SG averages for all four categories. Use the last 20 competitive rounds minimum for a reliable baseline.
  • 2
    Compare each category against the scratch baseline (0.0). A negative SG: APP of −0.3 means you are losing 0.3 strokes per round vs. scratch standard.
  • 3
    Convert to a plus target: if your goal is +1, you need to gain +0.35 in APP, +0.25 in OTT, +0.15 in ARG, and +0.10 in Putting above your current scratch baseline.
  • 4
    Prioritise the category with the largest gap per session-hour invested — almost always APP for scratch-level players. See the 12-Week Cycle tab for the full practice plan.

SG: APP — Proximity Compression

At plus-handicap level, the approach metric that matters is not hitting more greens — it is hitting closer when you do. The difference between scratch and +2 is approximately 5–9 feet of average proximity from the 125–175 yard zone.

🎯 Approach Efficiency — The Elite Metric
Beyond GIR

Proximity Compression Model

Approach efficiency is the ratio of your actual proximity to your expected proximity given starting yardage. A player who consistently hits to 20 feet from 150 yards when the benchmark is 27 feet has efficiency above 1.0 — outperforming distance expectations.

Elite targets: approach efficiency ≥ 1.15 from 100–150 yards, ≥ 1.10 from 150–175 yards. Use Arccos proximity data (Approach section) and cross-reference with the table above to identify your weakest zone.

🔬 125–175 Yard Focus Protocol
Highest SG Leverage Zone

Training the Key Distance Band

  • 1
    Identify your worst zone: Pull Arccos proximity data by 25-yard band. The band where your proximity most exceeds the benchmark is your primary training focus.
  • 2
    Trace to a technical cause: Excess proximity in a specific zone traces to either distance control (right number, wrong shape) or direction (correct distance, wrong line). Use Mevo carry data to identify which.
  • 3
    Build a zone-specific drill block: 20-minute deliberate practice sessions focused solely on one distance band, with proximity feedback using a target circle or measured flag.

SG: OTT — Width vs Distance

Tour data consistently shows plus-handicap amateurs lose more strokes through lateral dispersion than through distance deficiency. Being 10 yards shorter than benchmark costs far less than being 15 yards off-line. Elite tee play is a width problem, not a distance problem.

🔬 Monthly Dispersion Protocol
Mevo Measurement Session

Measuring Your True Envelope

  • 1
    15 driver shots at maximum effort, target at 250 yards. Record Mevo lateral offset for each shot using range balls (apply established correction factor).
  • 2
    Remove the single worst miss in each direction. Calculate remaining range — this is your 85th-percentile envelope.
  • 3
    Compare to benchmark table. If lateral dispersion exceeds target, check HackMotion face angle data — excess width at elite level almost always traces to face angle variance, not path variance.

Putting at Plus Level

For a scratch player, the remaining putting gains are concentrated in the 6–10 foot range — not lag putting, not tap-ins. The make percentage from inside 8 feet is the clearest single separator between scratch and plus-handicap putters.

The 6–10 foot range shows the steepest gradient between handicap levels. Lag putting (20 feet+) shows minimal differentiation at elite level — it is already near-optimal for serious amateurs.

"The difference between scratch and +2 on the greens is not reading — it is execution from 6–10 feet. Make% in that range is almost entirely a stroke-quality and pressure-management issue."

🏋️ 6–10 Foot Practice Protocol
Weekly 100-Putt Protocol

Closing the Plus-HCP Putting Gap

  • 1
    Gate drill (6 feet): 25 putts with tee gates 1 putter-head-width either side. Target: 78%+ make rate. Gate drills train face angle precision — the dominant variable at 6 feet.
  • 2
    Circle drill (8 feet): 8 balls in a circle at 8 feet. Complete the full circle without missing — restart if you miss. Target: complete within 3 attempts consistently.
  • 3
    Pressure drill (10 feet): 10 putts with a consequence for missing more than 4. Record actual vs. target make%. Pressure-specific execution is what distinguishes competition performance from practice performance.

Short Game at Plus Level

The plus-handicap ARG metric is not scrambling percentage — it is proximity from non-standard lies. The jump from scratch to +2 in ARG is achieved primarily by improving saves from tight lies, rough, and slopes at 15–30 feet, not by converting easy chips more reliably.

The largest gaps are on downslopes and tight lies — the two lie types most frequently encountered on links and heathland courses where national amateur events are held.

🏌️ Tight Lie Priority
Technical Requirements

Elite-Standard Tight Lie Execution

A tight lie demands precise dynamic loft at impact — the margin for error is 2–3mm of strike relative to the arc's lowest point vs. 8–10mm from a normal fairway lie.

  • 1
    Lead wrist position: HackMotion data should show 5–10° of extension at impact — less than a normal chip. Most scratch players are too cupped on tight lies.
  • 2
    Ball position: Move 1 inch further back than a standard chip — raises the effective strike point relative to arc's lowest point.
  • 3
    Practice surface: Use a firm rubber mat or doormat for 15 minutes per session. Firm surface feedback trains ball-first contact in a way a soft mat insert cannot.

📊 Re-Benchmark at Week 12
Quarterly SG Audit

Measuring What Changed

  • 1
    Pull last 20 competitive rounds from Arccos. Calculate SG averages for all four categories. Compare to Week 0 baseline.
  • 2
    If APP improved by less than −0.15 over 12 weeks, technical work is not transferring — review with your coach whether the issue is execution or decision-making.
  • 3
    Set the next 12-week focus based on the updated gap table. The category with the largest remaining gap becomes the next primary focus.
  • 4
    Update Progress Journal (Guide 17) with the re-benchmark data. The compound record of quarterly SG deltas is your most valuable long-term training asset.
⭐

Realistic expectations: −0.35 SG: APP in 12 weeks is achievable but demanding. Most scratch players see −0.15 to −0.25 in a focused cycle. If you hit the full target, the next cycle prioritises OTT. If you hit half, the next cycle stays in APP with a modified technical focus.
