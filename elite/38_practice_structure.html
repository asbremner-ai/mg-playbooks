What This Playbook Covers

The Missing Framework — How Skill Is Actually Built

Most practice plans tell you what to practise. This playbook tells you how to structure sessions so that what you practise actually transfers to the course under pressure. The research on this topic is among the most consistently replicated in motor learning — and almost completely absent from golf instruction.

Module 01

Blocked vs. Random Practice

Why hitting 50 7-irons in a row builds range confidence but not game competence — and exactly how to restructure sessions.

Module 02

Contextual Interference Effect

The counterintuitive finding that harder, messier practice produces better long-term retention than smooth, repetitive practice.

Module 03

Variability Training

How practising variable targets, lies, and distances accelerates skill transfer versus fixed repetition at a single target.

Module 04

Desirable Difficulties

The full framework of practice conditions that feel harder but produce faster long-term gains — spacing, interleaving, retrieval practice.

Module 05

Skill Stage Adaptation

How practice structure must change as skill develops — blocked is appropriate for beginners, random for skilled players. Most amateurs have this backwards.

Module 06

Practice-to-Course Transfer

The specific conditions that maximise transfer from range to course — consequence practice, simulation protocols, and competition preparation sessions.

Module 07

Session Design Templates

Ready-to-use session structures for technical work, integration practice, and competition simulation — with SG category targeting built in.

Module 08

Feedback & Self-Assessment

When to use launch monitor feedback, when to withhold it, and how the timing of feedback affects learning versus performance. The 1-in-3 feedback rule.
