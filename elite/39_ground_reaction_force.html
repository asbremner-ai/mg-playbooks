Why GRF Has Its Own Playbook

The Speed Source Most Amateur Programmes Ignore

GRF is referenced in the Long Game, Fitness, and Speed Training playbooks — but never given a coherent, unified treatment. This playbook consolidates everything: the biomechanics, how to diagnose your GRF pattern using only the tools you already own, and the specific training protocol for each phase of ground force production.

The Three GRF Phases
Phase 1 — Vertical Loading (trail foot pressure at the top)
Phase 2 — Lateral Drive (lead hip shifts toward target before rotation)
Phase 3 — Rotational Impulse (lead foot braking drives upper body acceleration)
Each phase has a specific feel cue, a Mevo proxy metric for indirect measurement, and a targeted drill. Tour players optimise all three — most amateurs skip Phase 2 entirely, producing the hip spin-out fault.
