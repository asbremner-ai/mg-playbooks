Complete EV Thinking — From Dispersion Data to Optimal Aim Point

The Course Management and Shot Dispersion playbooks introduce expected value thinking and the Decade framework. This playbook takes both to completion — giving you the full calculation methodology, worked examples for every scenario type, and a reusable pre-round planning template you can apply to any course you play.

The Core EV Formula
EV(aim point) = Σ P(landing zone i) × E(strokes from zone i)

Optimal aim point = the aim point that minimises EV(strokes to hole out)
NOT the aim point that maximises perfect-shot outcome
Tour caddies compute this intuitively — after 10,000 holes, the probabilities are internalised. This playbook gives you the explicit calculation so you can build the same intuition systematically, using your own Arccos data as the probability inputs.
Why "aim at the middle of the green" is almost always wrong

Green centre is optimal only when miss consequences are symmetrical — which they almost never are. A flag with a bunker left and rough right has asymmetric consequences. Your optimal aim point shifts right of centre. By how much? This playbook gives you the exact calculation.
