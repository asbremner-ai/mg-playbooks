The Problem

You're Probably Practising
the Wrong Thing

Strokes gained data from 200,000+ tracked rounds shows a consistent pattern: golfers' intuitions about where they're losing shots are almost always wrong. And practice built on wrong intuitions produces frustrating plateaus.

🎯

SG: Approach is the biggest lever — almost always

For most 5–18 handicappers, iron approach shots from 100–175 yards account for 40% of the scoring gap to scratch. Yet most golfers spend more time on the putting green.

🏌️

Double bogeys cost more than three-putts

One double bogey per round typically costs more strokes than two three-putts. Eliminating high-risk decisions is worth more than any technical improvement.

📊

Blocked practice doesn't transfer to the course

Hitting 100 balls at the same target builds range confidence. Research is clear that interleaved, contextual practice is what actually improves performance under pressure.

🔄

Without data, the same weaknesses persist

Without tracking, there's no way to know if practice is working. The improvement loop — lesson, range, round, repeat — has no feedback mechanism to prioritise correctly.

Questions

Common Questions

Do I need Arccos to use this?

No. The system works with any tracking method — Arccos, Shot Scope, Garmin (Approach R10/S series), Golfmetrics, Hole19, or even manual scorecard tracking. Guide 26 (Reading Your Data) has a decision tree for every setup, and the strokes gained methodology applies regardless of how you collect your data.

What handicap level is this for?

Standard is built for 5–25 handicap golfers with an improvement goal. The framework scales — the same SG prioritisation logic that tells a 20-handicapper to fix their short game tells a 5-handicapper to fix their approach irons. Pro adds depth for single-figure players; Elite is specifically for scratch and near-scratch.

Does this work outside the UK?

Yes — built on the World Handicap System (WHS), the global standard used in over 100 countries. Whether you play via GHIN (USA), Golf Australia (MiScore), Golf Canada, Golf South Africa, or any WHS affiliate, the strokes gained methodology and programme structure apply directly. The competitive pathway guides are UK-specific, but all technical content — swing mechanics, stats interpretation, practice structure, mental game, fitness — is universal. Pricing is in GBP; approximate conversions are shown on each paid tier above.

What format is the content delivered in?

HTML files — mobile-optimised, offline-capable (PWA), and designed to be added to your phone's home screen for one-tap access on the course. Standard is hosted online; Pro and Elite are delivered as a zip download to open locally. No app required, no subscription, no login.

Is there a refund policy?

30-day satisfaction guarantee on all paid tiers. If you've worked through the content and don't feel it was worth what you paid, request a full refund — no questions asked.
